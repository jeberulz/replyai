# Messaging Framework — ReplyPilot AI

> Built from brand-context.md, brand-positioning.md, brand-strategy.md, brand-voice.md, and target-audience.md. Taglines carry forward from brand-strategy.md section 12 rather than reinventing them, per the messaging philosophy of consistency.

## 01 — Core Message

*ReplyPilot AI helps founders and builders who grow on X find the conversation worth replying to before the window closes, by ranking discovery and timing signals instead of just generating text.*

## 02 — Value Proposition

ReplyPilot AI finds the X conversations worth a builder's next fifteen minutes — before the reply window closes — and hands them a reply that already sounds like them. It's built for founders, indie hackers, and AI builders who already know replies drive growth but don't have hours to scan X looking for the right one. The outcome is more replies that land, sent in less time, without editing generic AI text into something that sounds like them. What makes it different: every other tool in this category already solved writing; ReplyPilot AI solves finding the right moment, which nobody else touches.

## 03 — Tagline Options

**"Reply before the window closes."** — *Functional.* States the core mechanic (timing) directly, works at hero-headline length or social-bio length without editing. **Recommended** — it's the most economical, the most action-oriented, and it leads with the actual differentiator instead of the AI/writing angle.

**"Fifteen minutes. The right reply."** — *Functional.* Encodes the audience's real time-scarcity insight concretely; strongest runner-up.

**"Find it. Reply right. Move on."** — *Functional.* Mirrors the product's actual three-step flow in the same clipped rhythm as the brand voice.

**"The discovery layer voice-generation forgot."** — *Witty / differentiation-forward.* Stakes the positioning claim against Typefully/Tweethunter/Hypefury without naming them — sharper for internal/strategic use than a first-touch public headline, since it assumes the reader already knows the category.

**"Built for the two-hour window."** — *Functional / aspirational hybrid.* Specific enough to read as credible rather than salesy, but describes the brand rather than inviting action — better as a section subhead than a primary tagline.

## 04 — Messaging Hierarchy

**Level 1 — Brand Headline**: *Reply before the window closes.*

**Level 2 — Supporting Statement**: ReplyPilot AI ranks the conversations worth your next fifteen minutes and hands you a reply in your own voice — no more scanning, no more generic AI text to edit.

**Level 3 — Key Messages**

**Find the moment, not just the words**
Every other tool helps you write. ReplyPilot AI tells you which conversation is actually worth replying to, before it goes cold.
*Proof: Conversation Score ranks tweets by audience size, topic relevance, timing, and growth velocity.*

**Three options, not ten**
Decision fatigue kills quality faster than a small menu ever could — so you get three considered options with a reason each, not an overwhelming list.
*Proof: Every option includes a stated reason it's worth sending, not a fake confidence score.*

**Sounds like you, not like a tool**
Voice profiles are trained on your actual tweet history, so replies read as yours, not as generic AI output.
*Proof: Voice Training imports vocabulary, sentence length, formatting, and reading level from your real tweets.*

**You always click send**
No auto-publish, ever — a human decision on every single reply, which protects the account you've spent years building.
*Proof: Publishing requires an explicit click on that specific reply, every time, no exceptions.*

**Level 4 — Proof Points**
1. Conversation Score ranks tweets by audience size, topic relevance, reply timing, and growth velocity.
2. Three reply/quote-tweet options per request, with a "generate more" button — not ten by default.
3. Every option includes a stated reason it's worth sending; no fake engagement percentages until real data exists.
4. Voice profiles trained from your actual tweet history — vocabulary, sentence length, formatting, reading level.
5. Publishing always requires an explicit click on the specific reply — no automated sending, ever.
6. North star metric is percent of replies used with no or minor edits — not volume of replies published.
7. Built for a single account and workflow — not diluted for agency/multi-account use in v1.
8. Feed Scanner surfaces high-opportunity conversations from your following feed and lists, on a schedule you control.

## 05 — Audience-Specific Messaging

**For Maya-type builders (solo founders / indie hackers):**
- What they care most about: time — spending less of it while still showing up in the right conversations.
- How to lead the message: time scarcity and the reply window, never "AI-generated content quality."
- Proof points that land hardest: Conversation Score (saves scanning time), Voice Profile (sounds like her without editing), three-not-ten options (fast decisions).
- Avoid: leading with "AI-powered" framing — she's already tried tools that oversell the writing part and found it wasn't her bottleneck.

**For Dev-type builders (AI builders / small technical teams):**
- What they care most about: trust and account safety — skeptical of inflated metrics, worried about automation flags on accounts they've spent years building.
- How to lead the message: transparency (reasons, not scores) and the human-click-to-send guarantee.
- Proof points that land hardest: no fake-precision scores, mandatory manual send, Conversation Score with stated reasoning.
- Avoid: any confident-sounding stat without an explanation behind it — he'll notice immediately, and it costs trust permanently, not temporarily.

## 07 — Things Not to Say

- **Claims without proof**: any specific engagement percentage or score not backed by real usage data — explicitly banned by the product's own rules, not just a copy preference.
- **Industry clichés to avoid**: "AI-powered," "supercharge your growth," "10x your engagement," "unlock your potential" — the generic AI-tool marketing language this brand is positioned against.
- **Competitor-adjacent language that creates confusion**: never lead with "write better tweets" or "AI writing assistant" framing — that's the exact commodity ground Typefully, Tweethunter, and Hypefury already own. Leading with it makes ReplyPilot AI sound like a slower version of tools that already exist.
- **Anything that contradicts positioning**: no volume/growth-hacking framing ("post more, grow faster") — this directly conflicts with the quality-over-volume value. No messaging that implies agency/team readiness — v1 is explicitly single-account, and implying otherwise would set up an audience mismatch the product isn't built to serve yet.
